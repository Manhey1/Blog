Dr. Norman Borlung

The man who save a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlung, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to inrcease wheat yield.
This was part of his lifelong war on hunger

Here is a timeline of his achievements

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - leaves his family's farm to study at the University of Minnesota. This was possible because of the "National Youth administration" program, which ran during the Great Depression
  • 1935 - He had to stop school to save up money. He gets a job at the Civilian Conservation Corp, which helped starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me"
  • 1937 - He finishes school and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
Borlung's life and achievements are a testimony to his far-reeaching contibution
